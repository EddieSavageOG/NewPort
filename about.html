How I Got Started

I began building computers in 1993 when my mother (a quadralateral amputee) was given a computer as an accessibility device by the State of California. As was common at the time, her IBM 386 machine would break in a myriad of ways and the independent computer technician we paid to repair these issues took me on as her apprentice, teaching me to identify the different boards used in computers, how to assemble them into a working machine, and even write necessary system files from memory (config.sys, command.com, etc.). Soon, I had built my own 8088 and taken over the tech support at our home.

Later, I learned several different programming languages in high school (VBasic, Basic, Pascal, and C++), and then learned HTML, and later CSS on my own. Even though I chose not to go into Computer Science when I graduated high school, I continued teaching myself JavaScript and PHP independently for fun. I had fallen deeply in love with the internet - I spent hours and hours on local BBSes, maximized my time on AOL (sorry Mom and Dad!), Compuserve, and Prodigy.

As such, computers are an accessibility device first and foremost to me - a tool that makes our lives and work easier and more efficient, granting us access to knowledge, experiences, and tools that we may never otherwise have been able to access.
